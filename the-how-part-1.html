The how part 1

Date Fri 31 July 2015 Category history Tags vanity / archiving / recording

It hadn’t occurred to me to do anything with the pile of tapes I’ve been amassing and carrying around for getting on for thirty years, but when I sold my house in 2014 and stopped using it as a spare room while living in the house I now shared with my wife and daughter, they had to go somewhere. There was a pile of pre-recorded tapes that I had collected from the 80s and into the 90s before that technology died off, a smaller pile of independent tapes in duplicated sleeves and a collection of not-quite highest quality blank cassettes that contained the results of a year or so of intensive recording in the early 1990s.

The problem was how to get the music off them. Almost 25 years after the fact, Portastudios are rare things, museum pieces at best. To make things more complicated, the original device was the budget Porta One, which recorded at 1 7/8 inches per second, standard cassette speed, where the majority of Portastudios did 3 3/4 ips, double speed. So the search was on to find something to play the cassettes on.
