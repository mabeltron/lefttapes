About

Back in the early 90s I was stumbling towards something or other but having what seemed to be a long sojourn between university and real life back at home with my parents in Doncaster. A few jobs came and went but then I got lucky and got made redundant at the same time as a small savings account matured. Like any responsible young adult, I ran down to the music shop in town and bought a second hand Tascam Porta One, a Boss SE-50 effects box and a Korg DDD-5 drum machine. I more or less locked myself in my bedroom for a few months and pretty much worked a working day recording songs for what I hoped would one day become a band.

The band never came together, even as I moved to London to try and seek my fortune, but work and life got in the way, and the tapes ended up in a box that travelled with me for the next 20 years until I unearthed them last year. I found a slightly newer and better Tascam 464 on eBay and spent a few months digitising the tapes solely for my own enjoyment, but then it occurred to me to at least put them somewhere for posterity, so Left Tapes was born.
