LFT001: Mesmerists: Left Songs

‘Left Songs’ represents the band in my head in the early 90s. From finding like-minded people in Middlesbrough and failing to get things together to stay in the north-east, I found myself back in Doncaster, in a series of unfulfilling jobs that eventually petered out, but with a small cache of money from an endowment that I promptly spent on something I had been dreaming of for years - a Portastudio, an effects box and a drum machine.

With this small collection of hardware, and a couple of cheap guitars I recorded obsessively to get the songs that I had been amassing out of my head and on to tape. I recorded a song a day for a few months, often just starting from a bass or guitar figure. The figures were then structured with the drum machine and piled up on tape. Most songs were recorded with a four piece band in mind (well, three, I liked the idea of having a drum machine) so would be bass and two guitars playing intertwining parts.

I think influences are fairly clear: Joy Division and New Order, Lowlife, Kitchens of Distinction, some of the bands who fell into the shoegaze category, the Smiths and REM when chiming guitars were required. On the other hand I was also listening to Orbital and the Shamen and other techno acts and drifting off into other experimental areas which was at odds with the rock core of the music.

The tracks were mostly recorded between in 1991 and 1992, mostly in Doncaster, but some from when I had decided to move to London to seek my fortune, which I did, sort of, at the cost of the music, of which more later.
